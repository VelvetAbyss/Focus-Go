## Design Context

### Users
- Core users are individual knowledge workers and procrastination-prone users.
- They open Focus&go at the start of the workday and keep it open until end of day.
- The job to be done is to complete planning, focus execution, notes, and reflection in one place with minimal switching cost.

### Brand Personality
- 3-word personality: one-stop, convenient, efficient.
- Emotional goal: control and clarity through the full workday.
- Product voice should reduce activation friction so users can start immediately without setup burden.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Visual tone: warmth is an emotional feel (not a yellow-heavy style); calm, soft, and comfortable for long sessions.
- Reference: Notion (clarity and low-friction information handling).
- Anti-reference: any interaction pattern that makes simple tasks complex.
- Color direction: low-saturation palette centered on `#F5F3F0`; use `#3A3733` instead of pure black/near-black for text and dark accents.
- Theme expectation: both light and dark, with comfortable contrast and non-harsh backgrounds.

### Design Principles
1. Start-fast by default: every core action should be immediately available with minimal steps.
2. Complexity budget: simple tasks must stay simple; advanced capability should never block basic flow.
3. One-day cockpit: the interface should support continuous all-day use with low cognitive load.
4. Warm clarity: prefer low-saturation, calm visuals over high-contrast, high-intensity styling.
5. Control without pressure: show progress and status clearly, but avoid anxiety-inducing visual urgency.

### Accessibility Baseline
- Maintain keyboard-accessible primary flows.
- Keep visible focus states across interactive controls.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Avoid pure black / near-black to reduce visual harshness in extended use.
